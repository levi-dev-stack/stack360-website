## Register

brand

## Users

Technical and business decision-makers evaluating a software partner: startup founders needing
an MVP fast, SMEs modernizing legacy systems, and enterprise leaders (CTOs, VPs of Engineering,
product owners) planning large, low-downtime migrations. Vertical focus on Healthcare, FinTech,
and Logistics. They arrive skeptical, comparing agencies, and are looking for proof of
engineering depth before they'll start a conversation. The job to be done: quickly judge whether
Stack360 can architect and ship a complex system at their scale, then reach out.

## Product Purpose

Stack360 is a custom software architecture studio that designs and builds complex software
ecosystems — ERP, CRM, AI solutions, SaaS platforms, bespoke software, mobile/web apps, cloud,
DevOps, and automation. The site's job is to communicate senior engineering credibility and
convert qualified visitors into consultation/project inquiries. Success = a visitor trusts the
studio's technical judgment and starts a conversation ("Work With Us" / contact).

## Brand Personality

Precise, senior, and quietly confident — the engineer's engineer. Three words: **architectural,
rigorous, assured**. Voice is direct and technically fluent without jargon-for-show; it shows
mastery through specificity (metrics, systems, outcomes) rather than hype. Should evoke
confidence and competence, not playfulness or startup exuberance.

## Anti-references

- Generic "digital agency" template sites: stock hero + three identical icon-feature cards +
  logo soup + gradient blobs.
- Overhyped AI-startup landing pages that lead with buzzwords and empty superlatives.
- The saturated AI-slop look: tiny uppercase tracked eyebrows over every section, numbered
  section markers (01/02/03) as default scaffolding, gradient text, decorative glassmorphism.
- Cold, sterile enterprise-vendor pages that read as untrustworthy boilerplate.

## Design Principles

1. **Show the engineering, don't claim it.** Prove depth with real system metrics, architecture,
   and case-study evidence — not adjectives.
2. **Confidence through precision.** Exact numbers, concrete outcomes, and crisp language beat
   vague superlatives.
3. **One idea per fold.** Deliberate pacing; each section carries a single dominant message.
4. **Every surface is a credibility signal.** Motion, data viz, and craft themselves are the
   proof that the studio builds things well ("practice what you preach").
5. **Guide toward the conversation.** Every section should make starting a project feel like the
   obvious, low-risk next step.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.1 AA. Body text ≥4.5:1 contrast (watch muted grays on the tinted `neutral-100`
surface and light type on the dark `surface-dark` sections). Honor `prefers-reduced-motion` for
the hero, marquee, and animated charts with a crossfade/static fallback. Keyboard-navigable nav
dropdowns and visible focus states.
